# Data Enrichment Log

This log documents all the new records added to the unified financial inclusion dataset for Ethiopia. These additions aim to fill data gaps for the two core dimensions: **Access** (Account Ownership) and **Usage** (Digital Payment Adoption Rate).

---

- **Category**: regulation
- **Pillar**: *None* (unified event categorization principle)
- **Indicator**: NBE Payment Instrument Issuers Directive ONPS/01/2020
- **Status**: Implemented
- **Observation Date**: 2020-04-01
- **Source**: National Bank of Ethiopia
- **Source URL**: [NBE Directives](https://nbe.gov.et/)
- **Original Text**: "Payment Instrument Issuers Directive ONPS/01/2020 in April 2020"
- **Confidence**: High
- **Collected By**: Antigravity
- **Collection Date**: 2026-07-18
- **Notes**: This landmark regulation authorized non-banking institutions (like telecommunications operators) to issue mobile money, creating the legal pathway for Telebirr.

## 3. New Impact Links

### IMP_0015: Mobile Money Directive -> Mobile Money Account Rate
- **Parent Event**: `EVT_0011` (NBE ONPS/01/2020 Directive)
- **Pillar**: ACCESS
- **Related Indicator**: `ACC_MM_ACCOUNT`
- **Impact Direction**: increase
- **Impact Magnitude**: high
- **Lag Months**: 12
- **Evidence Basis**: The regulation is the direct legal enabler of non-bank mobile money operators, preceding Telebirr's launch.
- **Comparable Country Evidence**: Comparable to M-Pesa's growth in Kenya and Tanzania following regulatory approval.

### IMP_0016: DFS Liberalization Directive -> Mobile Money Account Rate
- **Parent Event**: `EVT_0012` (Foreign Operator Liberalization)
- **Pillar**: ACCESS
- **Related Indicator**: `ACC_MM_ACCOUNT`
- **Impact Direction**: increase
- **Impact Magnitude**: medium
- **Lag Months**: 10
- **Evidence Basis**: Direct cause for the entry of Safaricom and the launch of M-Pesa, which expanded customer choice.

### IMP_0017: Telebirr Sanduq Launch -> Digital Payment Adoption
- **Parent Event**: `EVT_0013` (Telebirr Sanduq)
- **Pillar**: USAGE
- **Related Indicator**: `USG_DIGITAL_PAYMENT`
- **Impact Direction**: increase
- **Impact Magnitude**: medium
- **Lag Months**: 6
- **Evidence Basis**: Launching savings and credit products on top of payment wallets increases wallet retention and payment activity.
- **Comparable Country Evidence**: Highly comparable to M-Shwari in Kenya and M-Pawa in Tanzania.
